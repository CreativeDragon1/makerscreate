01

What is this?

A focused YSWS for 3D-printable work.

MakersCreate gives participants a concrete goal: spend real time making objects that can actually be printed, reviewed, and shipped. The work can be artistic, mechanical, playful, or practical.

The site keeps the process legible. Every project has hours, notes, status, and a clear next step, so builders always know where they stand.

02

How it works

Five steps. No confusion.

  1. 01

    Sign up with Hack Club

    Use Slack OAuth to confirm who you are and create your participant record.

  2. 02

    Build a printable project

    Anything that can be printed counts, as long as the final result is genuinely 3D printable.

  3. 03

    Log your hours

    Add journal entries for each session so progress is visible and reviewable.

  4. 04

    Reach the target

    Once your approved hours cross the threshold, your account becomes eligible.

  5. 05

    Claim your printer

    Submit shipping details and wait for the printer to arrive.

03

Rules & eligibility

Simple constraints. Clear expectations.

Eligibility

  • Member of the Hack Club Slack community
  • Verified through Hack Club identity tooling
  • Not banned from Hackatime
  • Age range and shipping regions still to be finalized

Project rules

  • Every submitted project must be 3D printable
  • Hours are logged through the built-in journal
  • A public model link is required on submission
  • Minimum hours per project is still being finalized
Is this free?

Yes. Participants who complete the program requirements earn a free printer.

What counts as a 3D-printable project?

Anything that can realistically be printed and shared as a printable model.

What software can I use?

The brief does not limit software. Use the tools that help you ship the work.

How do I track my hours?

With project journal entries inside the site. Hours are reviewed before they count.

When do I get my printer?

After your approved hours reach the threshold and you submit shipping details.

What printer do I get?

The final printer model is still being decided.
